In 2022, I contributed to an article in press on the formation of oligarchies in the ubiquitous field of decentralised finance. We discuss the concentration and use of voting rights tokens. (Article is originally published in German.)

> DeFi supporters criticise the “fat cats” of the traditional financial world. However, they themselves are spawning a new breed of financial moguls, commonly referred to as “whales.” For example, whales can be the developers, advisors and early investors of a DeFi project who accumulate governance tokens before the general public can purchase them via crypto exchanges.
>
> DeFi is more like an oligarchy (more precisely a timocracy, in which rights are measured by assets): votes are sometimes decided by just four token holders (whales). The French historian and philosopher Alexis de Tocqueville pointed this out in the 19th century: “When a nation abolishes the aristocracy, centralisation naturally follows.” History seems to be repeating itself.
